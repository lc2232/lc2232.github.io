I've been meaning to start writing for a while. I find that writing is one of the best ways to solidify your own understanding of something, and I figure if my notes can help someone else along the way, even better.

## What I'll Write About

This blog is going to be a mix of things:

- **Project breakdowns** — Behind-the-scenes looks at things I've built and the decisions I made along the way
- **Book reviews** — Reviews of books I've read and found interesting
- **Career thoughts** — Reflections on transitioning from firmware to backend engineering

## A Bit of Background

I started my career in **firmware engineering**, writing embedded C for hardware devices, dealing with real-time operating systems, and IoT related internet protocols (specifically WiFi and Matter). It taught me a profound respect for the hardware that all of our software ultimately runs on, and the underlying systems needed to make it all work.

Over time, I became fascinated by the challenge of building software at *scale*, services that handle thousands of concurrent users, systems that stay up even when things go wrong, APIs that need to be fast, reliable, and maintainable. That's what pulled me towards backend and distributed systems engineering.

My current project is an investment dashboard that I am developing in my spare time which performs meta analysis on the Fund/ETF documentation and aggregates the data into a user friendly platform. Core technologies are Python, AWS, and various AI tools to assist with the analysis of the documentation. Stay tuned for a blog post once the first version is live!
